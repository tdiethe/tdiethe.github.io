SLIDE 8 MILTON — Early Disease Detection Case Study 1
  • Tool: MILTON — developed by Centre for Genomics Research
  • Combines genomic data + clinical biomarkers to detect early disease signals
  • Trained on 500k UK Biobank participants
  • Detects 1,000+ diseases up to 10–15 years before clinical diagnosis
  • Recognised by World Economic Forum as one of six transformative AI healthcare applications
  • Portal freely accessible — used in 95 countries
Talking points Early detection historically reliant on late-stage symptoms. AZ funded exome sequencing + WGS of all 500k UK Biobank participants (~£200M project). Open science commitment — portal publicly accessible.
SLIDE 10 AI in Biologics Discovery Case Study 2b
  • Shift from wet-lab DMTA cycle → in silico augmented workflows
  • New biologics leads identified in weeks not months
  • 10× improvement in antibody binding affinity demonstrated
  • 100% of biologics discovery pipeline is AI-assisted
  • InSide software for developability / CMC readiness screening
  • Increasing complexity (ADCs, multispecifics, cell therapies) plays to AI's strengths
Differentiator Data is the moat — decades of protein engineering data + deep screening platform. Scale → signal. Models learn from richer, more representative biology.
SLIDE 11 Antibody–Antigen: Technical Primer Technical
  • Epitope (antigen side) ↔ Paratope (antibody CDR loops) — ML predicts both
  • Physics-based docking augmented by ML (docking alone not sufficient — CAPRI results)
  • End-to-end complex prediction (epitope-paratope) — promising direction
  • In-silico developability assays
  • ML-assisted DNA libraries for smarter experimental design
Story to tell Multi-pronged approach. Deep collaboration with external KOLs (Segway etc.) — significant knowledge transfer accelerates in-house capability building. Pilots evaluating external partner claims rigorously.
SLIDE 12 Design–Build–Test–Learn Cycle Technical
  • Iterative cycle: Design → Build → Test → Learn
  • ML models update with each cycle; variant library expands
  • In silico screening sits inside the loop, reducing wet-lab burden
  • Affinity scores feed back to update models continuously
Visual cue Robot graphic illustrates automation. Emphasise the flywheel: better data → better models → better designs → more data.
SLIDE 13 Multi-Objective ML for Biologics Technical
  • Inputs: amino acid sequence, structure, chemical/physical/knowledge features
  • Pharmacology targets: binding affinity, specificity, species cross-reactivity
  • Developability targets: sequence liabilities, stability, yield, immunogenicity
  • Generative AI proposes new sequences for library design (the "D" of DBTL)
  • Truly multi-task: predict AND generate
Key framing Not a single-objective problem — simultaneous optimisation across pharmacology and developability axes. Generative models close the loop.
SLIDE 14 QCS — Computational Pathology Case Study 3
  • Tool: QCS (Quantitative Continuous Scoring) — proprietary AI pathology solution
  • Replaces subjective manual tumour biopsy analysis
  • Identifies patients most likely to respond to ADCs (TROP2 NMR biomarker)
  • 43% reduction in risk of disease progression or death (TROPION-Lung01)
  • FDA Breakthrough Device Designation for VENTANA® — first AI-driven computational pathology class 3 device
  • First ever AI-driven companion diagnostic approval candidate
Hook Every tumour is unique. Previous manual analysis: subjective, limited to human eye. Earlier breast cancer use: identified 20–40% more likely responders vs manual. New era of precision medicine.
